Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 20, 2026

Wondrilla ("we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy and is committed to protecting the personal information you share with us. This Privacy Policy explains how our organization collects, uses, securely stores, and occasionally discloses your data when you visit our website, utilize our donation platforms, or interact with our charity campaigns worldwide.

1. Detailed Information Collection

We collect information through multiple touchpoints to ensure the delivery of transparent services and donation processing. This includes:

  • Personally Identifiable Information (PII): This includes your first and last name, email address, physical mailing address, and phone number when you register as a volunteer, subscribe to our newsletter, or make a donation.
  • Financial and Payment Data: While we facilitate transactions, we never store sensitive payment details such as full credit card numbers or CVV codes on our servers. All financial operations are entirely handled by fully compliant third-party payment gateways (e.g., Stripe, PayPal) utilizing tokenized encryption protocols.
  • Automated Navigational Information: Upon visiting our website, we automatically collect basic browser metadata, IP addresses, referring domains, and operating system types to optimize user experience and site security.

2. Purpose and Utilization of Data

We strictly utilize the collected data to further our global relief initiatives and maintain communication with our generous donors. Specific use cases include:

  • Processing and authenticating monetary donations smoothly.
  • Issuing mandatory tax-deductible receipts and year-end financial summaries to your address.
  • Distributing quarterly impact reports, humanitarian crisis updates, and volunteer opportunities.
  • Internal analytical auditing to improve our website’s functionality and outreach effectiveness.

3. Cookie Policy and Tracking Technologies

Wondrilla's digital platform uses "cookies"—small data files placed onto your device—to establish session persistence, remember your language preferences, and aggregate anonymous traffic patterns. You may instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is sent. However, declining critical session cookies may disrupt your ability to utilize our donation widget effectively.

4. Information Sharing and Third-Party Disclosure

Wondrilla maintains a strict zero-tolerance policy against the commodification of donor data. We do not sell, rent, broker, or trade your personal information to third-party marketers.

We only share information under the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: We employ trusted third-party companies specialized in email distribution and cloud hosting to facilitate our operations. These providers are legally bound by strict confidentiality agreements.
  • Legal Compliance: We may disclose information if legally mandated by a valid subpoena, court order, or when acting in good faith to strictly comply with international anti-money laundering (AML) protocols.

5. Data Retention and Security Protocols

Your personal data is retained only for the duration necessary to fulfill the purposes scoped within this Privacy Policy or as demanded by strict tax and corporate retention laws. We safeguard databases utilizing enterprise-level firewalls, AES-256 server-side encryption, and enforced SSL/TLS transit channels.

6. Your Regulatory Rights (GDPR & CCPA)

Depending on your geographic jurisdiction, you encompass explicit rights over your personal data. You possess the continuous right to:

  • Request a comprehensive digital copy of the exact data we currently hold regarding you.
  • Mandate the correction of any inaccurate or outdated information in our systems.
  • Demand the total erasure ("Right to be Forgotten") of your personal data from our operational databases.

To exercise these rights, immediately contact our Data Protection Officer at privacy@wondrilla.org. We strive to process all data requests well within standard 30-day corporate mandates.
